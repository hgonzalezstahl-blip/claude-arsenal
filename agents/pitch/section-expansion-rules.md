# Section Expansion Rules — When to Show What

> Pitch reads this every time. Tells the agent which sections to expand, trim, or omit per application, and how to keep LinkedIn aligned without conflicting with tailored resumes.

---

## THE TWO-LAYER STRATEGY

The resume and LinkedIn play different roles:

| Surface | Purpose | Specificity | Update cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Resume** | Win the interview for ONE role | Narrow, tailored to JD | Per application |
| **LinkedIn** | Recruiter-discoverable umbrella | Broad, covers all target role types | Quarterly or on major milestones |

**Core rule:** the resume is always a **subset** of LinkedIn. Anything on the resume must be defensible from the broader LinkedIn picture. LinkedIn never contradicts the resume; the resume just goes deeper on the specific points that match the JD.

---

## CITIZENSHIP

- **Default: omit from resume.** U.S. norm. Application portals handle work-auth as a separate field.
- **Include when:**
  - JD explicitly states "U.S. citizens only" / "must have clearance" / "must be authorized"
  - Role is government, defense, federal contractor, or any clearance-track position
  - JD is for a regulated industry where work-auth is a hard filter (some banking, defense fintech, gov consulting)
- **Where to put it when included:** one short line near the bottom or under "Additional" — never at the top.

For Hector specifically: U.S. citizen. Surface only when the JD requires it.

## SELECTED APPLIED AI PROJECTS — when to include / expand

This section is Hector's biggest differentiator. Most process-engineering candidates don't ship their own AI tools. He does. But it's not always the right move to feature them prominently.

### Include and expand the AI projects section when:
- JD mentions GenAI, LLMs, AI implementation, AI/ML, machine learning, automation, digital transformation, intelligent automation, AI-enabled workflows
- Target company is tech-forward (FAANG, AI-native startups, modern SaaS, fintech with AI investment)
- Role is at the intersection of operations and AI (AI Operations, AI Program Manager, Applied AI, AI Implementation Lead)
- Role is at a senior IC or principal level where shipped portfolio matters
- The JD asks for "examples of AI projects you have built"
- Hector is competing against non-builder candidates and needs to differentiate

In these cases, expand to 3–5 AI projects, with one or two sentences of substance per project (problem solved + tech stack + outcome).

### Trim or omit the AI projects section when:
- JD is focused on traditional process engineering / Lean / Six Sigma without AI overlay
- Role is supply chain operations, logistics, or warehouse management at a non-tech-forward company
- 1-page resume target where space is at a premium and the section would crowd out core experience
- Role is sales / business development / customer-facing where AI projects are noise
- JD explicitly downweights side projects ("focus on enterprise experience")

In these cases, drop the section entirely or compress to a single line in Technical Skills ("Built and ship AI tooling — see linkedin.com/in/hector-gonz/projects").

### When in between, default to "include 2 projects":
The current Master Resume baseline is 2 projects (LeanLogic.org + Google Sheets Accounting Automation). That's a safe default for most mid-to-senior tech-adjacent applications.

- Home Depot: 5 bullets
- Dell Technologies: 3 bullets
- NKT Photonics: 3 bullets
- Fike Corporation: 2 bullets

This is a 2-page resume default. For tailoring:

### When to expand a role:
- It's the most relevant role to the JD (e.g., for a banking role, expand JPMorgan to 5–6 bullets and trim Home Depot to 3)
- The JD weights the kind of work done in that role
- The role contains the strongest evidence for a key JD requirement

### When to trim a role:
- It's older AND less relevant
- The 1-page target requires it
- The role is outside the JD's domain

Hard rule: the most recent role is rarely trimmed below 3 bullets. Older roles can go as low as 1–2 bullets when relevant.

## LINKEDIN STRATEGY — keep it umbrella, never tailored

The risk: tailored resumes contradict a too-specific LinkedIn → recruiters notice → trust erodes.

The solution: LinkedIn captures the *full breadth* of Hector's work, generically enough that any tailored resume can be defended against it.

### LinkedIn HEADLINE — don't over-specify

| Avoid | Use |
|---|---|
| "Senior Process Engineer at JPMorgan Chase" | Restricts to one identity |
| "AI Process Engineer" | Too narrow |
| **"Business Transformation & Applied AI Leader \| Process Engineering \| Lean Six Sigma Black Belt \| GenAI Implementation"** | Covers all target role types |

The headline should match the resume's positioning tagline at the umbrella level. Use pipe separators and 4–6 broad descriptors that span the role types Hector targets.

### LinkedIn ABOUT section — cover the breadth

A 3–4 paragraph "About" that:
- States the umbrella positioning (not narrowed to one role)
- Names the major impact areas (operations, supply chain, AI implementation, business transformation)
- References the M.S. in AI Management & Policy as the forward-looking signal
- Includes a soft CTA (open to roles in / interested in conversations about [list 3–4 broad role categories])

Avoid: any specific dollar metric that might appear differently on a tailored resume. LinkedIn metrics should be **scope-level** ("$100M program portfolio", "global retail footprint", "19-DC distribution network") rather than outcome-level ($X saved, Y% reduction). The resume goes specific; LinkedIn stays scope.

### LinkedIn EXPERIENCE — same companies, dates, titles, fuller bullets

Rules:
- **Identical companies, exact same titles, exact same dates** as every tailored resume — never deviate
- **More bullets per role on LinkedIn** (LinkedIn has no length limit) — pull from the full Verified Bullets Bank in master-cv.md so all major work is visible
- **Slightly more general phrasing** — describe the *scope* of the achievement (what you owned, what changed), but soften the precise dollar / % metrics where possible. Use ranges or scope ("led a multi-site program saving $1M+ annually") instead of exact figures ("saved $1.2M annually") when the LinkedIn version exists alongside multiple resumes that frame the same number differently.
- **Never contradict a resume bullet.** If the resume says "$950K transportation savings", LinkedIn shouldn't say "$1.5M transportation savings". They can both say "led the integration of an AI-enabled YMS, generating significant transportation cost savings" — the resume gets specific, LinkedIn stays scope.

### LinkedIn FEATURED section — show the AI portfolio

This is where Hector can show off everything without crowding tailored resumes:
- LeanLogic.org link
- GitHub or live demos for any AI projects
- Articles he's written or been quoted in
- Conferences / talks
- Awards (Customer Focused Award, etc.)
- Certifications (Lean Six Sigma Black Belt cert, future certs)

The Featured section is the **portfolio** that resumes can reference but not list in full.

### LinkedIn SKILLS — list everything

LinkedIn skills are searchable — every skill is a potential recruiter match. List broadly:
- All technical skills from master-cv.md (GenAI, Python, SQL, Power BI, Tableau, Agile, etc.)
- All domain skills (Lean, Process Mapping, ERP, YMS, Supply Chain, etc.)
- All soft skills relevant to senior roles (Cross-Functional Leadership, Change Management, Vendor Negotiation, Stakeholder Management, etc.)

LinkedIn shows the top 3 prominently — get those endorsed by colleagues so they rank high. The rest are searchable.

### LinkedIn PROJECTS section — list every AI project

Every applied AI project Hector has built goes here, with:
- Project name + 1–2 sentence description
- Tech stack used
- Outcome (in scope / scale terms, not specific dollar metrics)
- Link if there is one (LeanLogic.org, GitHub, demo)

This lets Pitch surface 2 projects on a tailored resume while the full portfolio lives on LinkedIn for any recruiter who clicks through.

### LinkedIn PROFILE PHOTO and BACKGROUND
- Professional headshot (recent, clear, neutral background)
- Background image: subtle, on-theme (avoid generic stock photos; consider something that signals the AI / transformation focus — a dashboard, a workspace, an industrial setting)

These are quality signals that compound. Out of scope for Pitch directly, but worth noting in fit summaries when a strong recruiter-facing presence matters.

## "WHEN TO EXPAND" CHEAT SHEET FOR PITCH

| Situation | Expand | Trim / Omit |
|---|---|---|
| JD mentions AI / GenAI / LLM | AI Projects section to 3–5 | (no trim) |
| JD is pure traditional ops / supply chain | (no expand) | AI Projects to 0–1 line in Skills |
| JD weights Six Sigma / Lean | Promote LSS Black Belt to Core Competencies and Signature Achievements | (no trim) |
| 1-page resume target | (none) | Older roles to 2 bullets, Signature Achievements to 3, AI Projects out, Summary to 3 sentences |
| 2-page resume target | Most-relevant role to 5–6 bullets | Less-relevant roles to 2–3 bullets |
| Government / defense / clearance role | Citizenship line added | (no trim) |
| Senior IC / principal level | AI Projects expanded, Featured-equivalent breadth | (no trim) |
| Sales / BD / customer-facing role | NKT + Fike to top with regional / language emphasis | AI Projects to minimum |
| Spanish / LATAM-relevant role | Add Languages line, expand NKT and Fike multilingual training | (no trim) |
| Recent grad / academic | Education with coursework | (no expand of older roles) |
