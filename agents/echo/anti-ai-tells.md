## STRUCTURAL PATTERNS TO AVOID

### Em-dash overuse
AI sprinkles em dashes (—) constantly to interrupt sentences with elaboration. Use them sparingly — at most 2-3 times per 1000 words. Most em-dash interrupters can be replaced with periods, commas, or parentheticals (or just cut).

### Tricolons everywhere
"X, Y, and Z" lists where the items are perfectly parallel and rhythmically balanced. Real writing has lopsided lists, two-item lists, and lists that break their own pattern. AI loves the rule of three to a fault.

### Parallel sentence openings
Three sentences in a row starting with the same word or grammatical structure. AI does this constantly. Vary the opening word every 2-3 sentences.

### Symmetric paragraphs
AI defaults to paragraphs that are all 3-4 sentences long, all roughly the same length, all with topic sentence + support + transition. Real writers vary wildly: 1-sentence paragraphs, 8-sentence paragraphs, fragments used for emphasis.

### Bullet points where prose belongs
AI bulletizes everything to avoid committing to sentence structure. Reserve bullets for genuinely list-shaped content. If the items have logical flow between them, write prose.

### Header parallelism
"Building Strong Foundations / Cultivating Growth / Embracing the Future" — three headers, all gerund phrases, all the same length. Dead giveaway. Vary header structure.

### Recap conclusions
AI conclusions summarize what the body just said. Real writing either ends with a punch (a final claim, a turn, an image) or just stops. Avoid "In this piece, we explored…" closers.

### "Not only… but also"
Heavy AI signal. Almost always a sign of artificial parallelism. Rewrite as two simple sentences.

### Excessive emphasis
**Bolding** every key term. Italicizing for emphasis multiple times per paragraph. Real writers emphasize sparingly.

## TONAL PATTERNS TO AVOID

### Cheerleader voice
"Exciting", "amazing", "fantastic", "wonderful", "thrilled" — used as default intensifiers regardless of the actual material. Real writers calibrate enthusiasm to the actual stakes.

### Wisdom voice
"True success lies in…", "The key to X is Y", "What truly matters is…" — generic aphorism mode. Cut or replace with a specific claim.

### Forced positivity
AI defaults to optimistic framing even when neutral or negative is more accurate. Don't soften real critique. Don't manufacture upsides.

### Over-explaining
Defining terms the reader knows. Stating the obvious. Walking through reasoning the reader can do themselves. Trust the reader.

### Hedging in stacks
"Some might argue that, in certain contexts, it could potentially be the case that…" — kill the stacked hedges. Pick one or none.

## WHAT TO DO INSTEAD

When you flag a tell, don't just delete it — replace it with something specific to the topic. Generic phrasing creates the AI smell. Specific, concrete, slightly idiosyncratic phrasing kills it.

**Examples:**

| AI tell | Specific replacement |
|---------|---------------------|
| "Plays a crucial role in driving growth" | "Drives ~30% of revenue" / "Is the reason the team hit Q3" / cut entirely |
| "Navigate the complexities of" | "Deal with" / "Work through" / "Figure out" |
| "Leverage cutting-edge technology" | name the actual technology |
| "Harness the power of AI" | "Use Claude" / "Use GPT-4" / "Use the model" |
| "It's important to note that" | just state the thing |
| "In today's fast-paced world" | name the actual context |
| "A testament to their dedication" | the specific evidence of dedication |

The pattern: **swap abstraction for specificity.** That alone removes most of the AI smell.
